🎯 Official Cornhole Rules

Gibs Juneteenth Cornhole Tournament — {{ event_date_str }}

Equipment & Setup

  • Each board is 2 ft × 4 ft with a 6-inch hole centered 9 inches from the top.
  • The hole is elevated 12 inches off the ground at the front edge.
  • Boards are placed 27 feet apart (front edge to front edge) on flat ground.
  • Each team uses 4 bags of a single color (corn-filled, ~1 lb each).

Teams & Players

  • Teams consist of exactly 2 players.
  • In doubles play, one player from each team stands at each board, facing their partner.
  • Partners always throw toward the opposite board.

Gameplay

  • A coin flip determines which team throws first.
  • Players alternate throwing bags — one team member throws, then the opposing player throws, continuing until all 8 bags are thrown (4 per side).
  • Players must stay behind the front edge of the board (the pitcher's box) when releasing a bag.
  • Bags must be thrown in an underhand motion.
  • A bag that bounces off the ground and lands on the board does not count and is removed before scoring.

Scoring

  • Cornhole (hole-in): 3 pts — bag goes through the hole.
  • Woody (on the board): 1 pt — bag lands and stays on the board surface.
  • Cancellation scoring is used: subtract the lower team's points from the higher team's points each round. Only one team scores per round.
  • Example: Team A scores 5, Team B scores 3 → Team A earns 2 points that round.

Winning

  • The first team to reach or exceed 21 points wins the game.
  • You do not need to win by 2 — hitting 21 exactly or going over wins.
  • Tournament uses a single-elimination bracket. Win your match to advance; lose and you're out.
  • All bracket matches are best-of-one game except the Final, which is best-of-three.

Fouls & Sportsmanship

  • Stepping past the front edge of the board while releasing = foul (bag is removed).
  • Distracting or taunting opponents is unsportsmanlike. Repeated violations may result in a forfeit at the tournament director's discretion.
  • Bags may not be moved or touched until the round is fully scored.
  • Disputes are settled by the tournament director — their decision is final.

Other Games (Free Play)

While cornhole is the featured tournament game, the following are available as free-play activities throughout the day at your convenience:

  • 🍺 Beer Pong
  • 🥤 Flip Cup
  • 🤝 Team Chug
  • 🎉 And more!

Please drink responsibly. No one under 21 is permitted to consume alcohol.
